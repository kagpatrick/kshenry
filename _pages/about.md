**About Me**

Dr. **Kitiabi Sohre** is a public health researcher committed to advancing data-driven approaches for improving population health outcomes. Her work integrates epidemiology, biostatistics, and global health perspectives to address infectious disease surveillance, health equity, and adolescent HIV prevention.

She earned her **Ph.D. in Epidemiology and Biostatistics** from the University of Georgia, where her dissertation examined HIV testing behaviors, PrEP awareness, and behavioral risk factors among U.S. adolescents and young adults. Through her training and research experience, Dr. Kitiabi has developed deep expertise in study design, data analysis, and translating epidemiologic evidence into actionable public health insights.

Dr. Kitiabi’s professional experience spans academic, government, and international development settings—including the Georgia Department of Public Health, CARE International, and the University of Georgia’s College of Public Health. Across these roles, she has contributed to multi-institutional research on tuberculosis, HIV, and digital health innovations in Africa and the U.S., emphasizing data integrity, implementation science, and policy relevance.

As a dedicated educator and mentor, Dr. Kitiabi also serves as a **Graduate Teaching Assistant** in Epidemiology, where she helps students connect theory with practice through applied coursework, field investigations, and data interpretation. Her career vision is to leverage evidence and technology to strengthen global public health systems and promote equitable access to health innovations.
